もんだいようしを
もんだい（問題）: "Mondai" means "question" or "problem" (as in a test question or an issue to solve).
ようし（用紙）: "Youshi" means "paper" or "form" (a sheet of paper used for a specific purpose).
「もんだい + ようし」 is a noun + noun combination. In Japanese, the first noun modifies the second noun, meaning the main idea is determined by the second noun.
The particle を is a direct object marker in Japanese. It indicates the object of an action in a sentence.
を + Verb: The particle を marks what the action is being done to.
It must be followed by an action verb (e.g., 読む (read), 書く (write), もらう (receive), 捨てる (throw away)).
を is only used with transitive verbs (verbs that take a direct object).
